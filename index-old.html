I am currently a Ph.D. candidate under Prime Minister's Research Fellow (PMRF) scheme at IIT Delhi advised by Prof. Subir Kumar Saha. My current research interests lie in the intersection between physics- and AI-based modeling and control of robotic systems, addressing topics such as robot model learning, robot manipulation, and physical human-robot interaction (pHRI). This research is aimed towards intelligent and physically consistent robots that interact seamlessly with humans. I am also passionate about developing autonomous systems for use in medicine, particularly for remote diagnosis of patients and precise surgery.

Previously, I was a researcher in TCS Robotics Research and Innovation Lab (Delhi), contributing towards the automation of warehouse industry across the country and globe. I obtained M.Tech at IIT Jodhpur advised by Prof. Suril V. Shah. My masters research focused on space robotics, addressing autonomous on-orbit servicing (OOS) operations such as capture of debris and servicing of satellite using a multi-arm robot.

Research

Ph.D. Research

Background and Problem setup. We are entering into an era of Human-Robot Collaboration (HRC) where robots assist humans to collaboratively achieve complex tasks. We can envision a future where robots possess human-like manipulation abilities and proactively assist us in homes, hospitals, and factories. To be useful as a teammate and work safely in human environments, a robot must possess the intelligence to adapt to various physical interactions in response to different task requirements and stochastic disturbances. Intelligent robotic teammates have the potential to expand human capabilities, reduce the risk of harm, increase productivity, and ultimately the quality of life for our people. Artificial Intelligence (AI) is being considered as a key to the next wave of this robotics revolution. However, AI techniques require a huge amount of data and this is not always feasible in HRC scenarios. Having worked on both physics- and AI-based approaches, I believe that the solution to this new and exciting revolution of robotics i.e. human-robot teaming, relies on a proper blend of advancements in AI and classical approaches of robot modeling. Therefore, my vision is to bridge the gap between these two fields and push the resulting interdisciplinary research in a direction to tackle fundamental challenges in the HRC.

Proposed Research. My research goal is to develop a methodology to provide algorithmic solutions for making intelligent decisions in the challenging physical human-robot interaction (pHRI) scenarios applicable in medical and industrial settings for the benefit of humanity. To achieve this goal, fundamental research in Machine Learning (ML), Reinforcement Learning (RL), robot dynamics modeling, force control of robots will be carried out and unified to realize an intelligent architecture for use-cases of pHRI. The traditional methods for robot modeling and control were derived from laws of physics. These approaches provide mathematical performance guarantees but modeling complex systems is not always possible. On the other hand, state-of-the-art AI approaches such as Machine learning (ML) or Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) allow for automatic learning of models or skills from data. However, these approaches don’t provide mathematical guarantees, require a huge amount of data, slow to learn and often generalize poorly to new situations. In this thesis, we present a combination of both by infusing domain knowledge from classical approaches into AI learning methods. I plan to explore new directions to address fundamental questions, including:

  • How can we infuse the domain knowledge of robot models or environment into AI methods?
  • What kind of domain knowledge needs to be incorporated to accurately capture the dynamics of complex manipulation skills?
  • How the proposed robot modeling framework can quickly adapt to the changes in the environment during physical interaction?
This research will result in a framework that can accurately learn physically plausible models, improve the learning speed and generalization ability of robotic skills in collaborative tasks.

Current Work. I started out my work by studying the fundamentals of state-of-the-art AI approaches and implemented them for robot model and skill learning.

Robot model learning is the process of acquiring robot models from data. The efficacy of model-based control depends heavily on the quality of the model used to predict how control inputs affect the dynamics of the robot. While system identification methods can be used to improve the accuracy of a model, models derived from first-principles often fail to accurately express complex phenomena such as friction or aerodynamic drag, suffering from model bias. To avoid such bias, we used a deep neural network to represent robot dynamics where their parameters have been trained using data collected from the 2-link robotic system, as shown in figure below. The computational efficiency of the learned model is found to be better than analytical models, but the major drawback of learned models is the requirement of a large amount of data to train.

Robot skill learning To interact seamlessly with the environment, robot need to possess many skills and among them, target reaching is the first and most fundamental task. In this, a 2-link robot has been trained to reach a random target in its workspace by implementing state of the art deep RL algorithms like Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (DDPG) and Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO). The task is demonstrated in animation below, where red circle is the target and the green circle represents the end-effector of 2-link robot; this is where the hand or a gripper is on a real robot.

Telerobotic Ultrasound of COVID-19 patients. This is a collaborative project between IIT Delhi, AIIMS and Addverb Technologies. We developed a simulation and hardware platform for the remote ultrasound of COVID-19 patients using a Geomagic touch haptic device at doctor's site and UR5 robot at the patient's site. The schematic is shown in figure below. The article on this work was recently accepted in Workshop on Autonomous System in Medicine, International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS) 2020. This project is currently ongoing and we will be including human trials with ultrasound image feedback as a next step.


Future Work. I anticipate three main future directions towards my research goal: (1) developing a framework for hybrid learning of robotic skills involving interaction with the environment, providing data efficiency and theoretical guarantees, (2) formalizing fast adaptation techniques for better generalization of learned skills to unseen tasks, and (3) demonstrating the same on simulation and hardware platform for testing the effectiveness of the framework.
